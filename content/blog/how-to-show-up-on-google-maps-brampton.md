## What Is the Google Maps Local Pack and Why Does It Matter for Trades Businesses?

The Google Maps local pack appears in 93% of searches with local intent ([BrightLocal](https://www.brightlocal.com/resources/local-seo-statistics/), 2025). It is the block of three business listings that appears near the top of Google results when someone searches for a service in a specific area. Each listing shows the business name, star rating, review count, address, hours, and a link to their website. It is the most valuable piece of real estate on the results page, and the businesses in those three spots get the overwhelming majority of clicks.

For trades businesses in Brampton, this matters more than almost any other marketing channel. Local searches convert at a 28% rate, one of the highest conversion rates of any marketing channel ([Ranktracker](https://www.ranktracker.com/blog/local-seo-statistics-2025/), 2025). People searching for a roofer or a plumber are usually ready to call. Getting in front of them at that moment is where the work gets booked.

The good news is that Google Maps rankings can be influenced through specific, learnable actions. You do not need to outspend anyone. You need a well-optimized profile, consistent information across the web, strong reviews, and a website that confirms your location and services.

## Step 1: Claim and Fully Complete Your Google Business Profile

Your Google Business Profile is the single most important factor in your Google Maps ranking. Businesses with a complete profile are 2.7 times more likely to be considered reputable, 70% more likely to receive a visit, and 50% more likely to get a customer to consider purchasing from them ([Google](https://support.google.com/business/answer/10515606)).

If you have not claimed your profile yet, go to [google.com/business](https://www.google.com/business) and claim or create your listing. Google will mail a postcard with a verification code to your Brampton address.

Once verified, fill in every field:

- **Business name** — use your exact legal or operating name, nothing extra
- **Primary category** — choose carefully (e.g., "Plumber," "Roofing Contractor," "HVAC Contractor"). Add secondary categories where relevant.
- **Address** — your real Brampton address. If you travel to clients, you can hide your address and list service areas instead.
- **Service areas** — add Brampton, Mississauga, Caledon, and any other Peel Region cities you serve
- **Phone number** — your direct business line
- **Website** — link to your website
- **Hours** — keep these current, especially around holidays
- **Services** — list every service you offer with a short description
- **Description** — write a clear paragraph saying what you do, where you do it, and who you serve

The more complete your profile, the stronger the signal to Google that your business is real, active, and relevant for searches in Brampton.

## Step 2: Build Your Review Count and Protect Your Star Rating

Reviews are one of the strongest ranking factors in Google Maps. They also directly determine whether people choose to call you over a competitor. Ninety-seven percent of consumers read reviews before visiting a local business ([BrightLocal](https://www.brightlocal.com/resources/local-seo-statistics/), 2026), and 38% say a business needs a minimum average rating of 4.0 before they consider it trustworthy ([BrightLocal](https://www.brightlocal.com/research/local-consumer-review-survey-2025/), 2025).

For a trades business in Brampton, here is how to build reviews consistently:

1. **Ask right after the job is done.** The best time to ask is the moment a client says they are happy. A simple "Would you mind leaving us a quick Google review? It really helps" is enough.
2. **Send a follow-up text with a direct link.** Use the review link from your Google Business Profile dashboard. Texting it removes all friction — the client does not have to search for you.
3. **Ask every time, not just on the big jobs.** A five-star review from a small maintenance call counts the same as one from a full installation.

Do not ignore reviews once you get them. Eighty-eight percent of consumers say they would use a business that responds to all its reviews, compared to only 47% for businesses that never respond ([BrightLocal](https://www.brightlocal.com/research/local-consumer-review-survey-2025/), 2025). Reply to every review — positive ones with a genuine thank-you, negative ones with a calm, professional response that shows you take customer experience seriously.

Most trades businesses in Peel Region have fewer than 15 Google reviews. If you reach 25 reviews with a 4.5+ average, you are ahead of most local competition on that metric alone.

## Step 3: Make Your Website Support Your Local Ranking

Your website and your Google Business Profile work together. Google compares what is on your website against what is in your GBP to determine whether your business is relevant for searches in Brampton.

Your website needs to clearly state:

- Your business name, address, and phone number (NAP) — put this in your footer on every page
- The services you offer, with a clear section or page for each
- The cities you serve — Brampton, Mississauga, Caledon, Bolton, and the wider GTA if you cover them
- Schema markup — structured data that tells Google exactly what your business does and where (any good web developer should include this in your build)

NAP consistency is critical. If your website says "ABC Plumbing Inc." and your Google Business Profile says "ABC Plumbing," those are different in Google's eyes. Keep your business name, address, and phone number exactly the same across every online property.

## Step 4: Add Photos to Your Google Business Profile

Photos directly affect how many people engage with your listing and how often Google surfaces it. Businesses with photos on their GBP receive 42% more requests for driving directions and 35% more clicks to their website compared to businesses without photos ([Google](https://searchendurance.com/google-business-profile-statistics/), 2025).

For a trades business, the most effective photos to add are:

- **Finished job photos** — before and after shots, or strong finished-project images
- **Your team on the job** — even one or two photos of you or your crew working builds trust immediately
- **Your vehicle or equipment** — a branded van or truck with your name on it is worth photographing
- **Your logo** — set as your profile photo so your brand is recognizable in search results

Aim for at least 10 photos to start, then keep adding. Google rewards active profiles. The more recently you have uploaded photos or made updates, the fresher your listing appears in the algorithm.

## Step 5: Get Your Business Listed in Local Directories

A citation is any online mention of your business name, address, and phone number. Google uses citations as a trust signal — if your NAP appears consistently across multiple reputable directories, it tells Google your business is established and real.

The most important citation sources for trades businesses in Brampton are:

- **HomeStars** — the dominant home services review platform in Canada
- **Yelp** — claim and complete your listing
- **Yellow Pages Canada** — still heavily indexed by Google
- **Better Business Bureau (BBB)**
- **Facebook Business Page**
- **Houzz** — relevant for renovation, landscaping, and design trades
- **LinkedIn Company Page**
- **Local Brampton directories** — the Brampton Board of Trade and any local chamber listings if applicable

The rule that applies everywhere: your business name, address, and phone number must be exactly the same on every listing. One inconsistency does not collapse your rankings, but many inconsistencies add up. If your phone number or address changes, update every listing you have.

Most local trades businesses in Brampton have between 3 and 8 online citations. Getting to 20 or more consistent, accurate citations puts you ahead of competitors who have not touched this.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I need a physical address in Brampton to show up on Google Maps?

Not necessarily. Google allows service area businesses — contractors who travel to clients — to hide their physical address and list a service area instead. You can set Brampton and surrounding Peel Region cities as your service area and still appear in Maps results for those areas, even if you work from a home office or a shop outside the city.

### How many Google reviews do I need to rank in the local pack?

There is no set minimum, but review count and average rating are both ranking factors. In competitive Brampton categories like plumbing and HVAC, businesses in the top 3 typically have 25 or more reviews with a 4.5+ average rating. In less competitive trades categories, fewer reviews may be enough. Start asking for reviews immediately and build the habit into every job.

### Does my website affect my Google Maps ranking?

Yes. Google uses your website as a supporting signal alongside your GBP. A website that clearly lists your Brampton address, phone number, and services — with schema markup and consistent NAP — strengthens your local ranking. A website with mismatched information or no local signals can actively work against you.

### What is the fastest thing I can do today to improve my ranking?

Fully complete your Google Business Profile if you have not already. Add every service, set your service areas to cover Brampton and Peel Region, upload at least five photos, and make sure your business name, address, and phone number exactly match your website. That single step puts most Brampton trades businesses ahead of competitors with incomplete profiles.

### Does posting updates on my Google Business Profile help?

Yes, to a degree. Google Posts — short updates or offers published directly to your GBP — signal an active, maintained profile. Posting once a week or once every two weeks keeps your listing fresh and gives potential customers something current to read. It is not the most powerful ranking factor on its own, but it contributes to the overall activity signal Google looks for.
